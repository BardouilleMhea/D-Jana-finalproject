The Evolution of Computers.

The First Computer.


The first mechanical computer was created in 1822 by Charles Babbage. He called it The Babbage Difference Engine.

The ABC (Atanasoff Berry Computer) was the basis for the modern computer we all use today. The ABC weighed over 700 pounds and used vacuum tubes.

It had a rotating drum, a little bigger than a paint can, that had small capacitors on it.










The capacitor is a device that could store an electric charge like a battery.

The ABC was designed to solve problems with equations upto 29 different variables.

These are common problems in physics and other sciences which are difficult to solve by hand.


The ABC completed an operation about every 15 seconds, which is a lot slower than the computers we use which completes millions

of operations per second.

Unlike modern day computers we have today, the ABC had no change-able stored program. This meant the program was only designed to

do a single task. It also meant for these to be solved, an operator had to write down the answer and then feed it back

to the ABC.


The computers back then were more complex and harder to use whenever you wanted to. Nowadays most computers can be used as a carry on and they

are easier to understand how to use them.

Present day computers are easier to get than back then since it was very hard and expensive to get your hands on a computer unlike today.
